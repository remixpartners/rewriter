## The Scrub

Scan the draft for each pattern. Fix every instance.

Negative parallelisms. "It's not X, it's Y." "Not just X, but Y." "Not only... but also." AI uses this rhetorical move constantly. It can span two sentences. State the positive claim directly. Cut the theatrical contrast.

Rule of three. Lists of exactly three adjectives, benefits, or takeaways. "Innovative, transformative, and groundbreaking." AI defaults to triplets. Vary list lengths. Use two, four, or one. Or write a sentence instead of listing.

Em dash overuse. Em dashes where commas, parentheses, or colons would be more natural. LLMs were trained on novels, where em dashes are common. AI uses them in places where a human would choose a comma or parenthesis. Replace most. Note that AI also skips en dashes, using hyphens for ranges (1990-2000) where en dashes belong.

Formatting overkill. Excessive bolding. The "Term: Definition" bullet pattern. Numbered lists where prose works. Emojis in headers. AI reaches for formatting when it should write a sentence. Strip to the minimum needed for comprehension.

Cursed vocabulary. delve, intricate, tapestry, pivotal, underscore, landscape, foster, testament, enhance, crucial, multifaceted, nuanced, groundbreaking, transformative, paramount, leverage, streamline, synergy, spearhead, embark. Replace with plain words. "Important" instead of "crucial." "Complex" instead of "intricate." "Start" instead of "embark."

Cursed phrases. "Rich tapestry." "In the realm of." "It's worth noting that." "At the forefront of." "Serves as a testament to." "Plays a crucial role." "Paving the way for." "A beacon of." "Deeply rooted in." "Nestled in the heart of." Delete or rewrite with evidence.

Promotional tone. Writing that reads like a travel brochure or press release. "Rich cultural heritage." "Stunning natural beauty." "Breathtaking." LLMs default to advertisement-like writing even when prompted for neutrality. Replace emotional claims with specific facts. Don't tell the reader something is impressive. Describe it and let them decide.

Editorial commentary. "It's important to note." "It is worth noting." "No discussion would be complete without." "It cannot be overstated." Delete. If the point matters, the writing makes that clear without announcing it.

Compulsive summaries. "In summary." "In conclusion." "Overall." "Taken together." AI restates what was just said even when the passage is too short to need it. Delete. The piece ends. It does not conclude.

Hedging pileups. Almost, apparently, comparatively, fairly, somewhat, sort of, to some extent, "I would argue," rather, relatively, seemingly, presumably. AI hedges everything to the same degree, flattening the natural variation in confidence that human writers display. Cut the hedge or commit to the claim.

Transition survivors. Moreover, furthermore, in addition, on the other hand, notably, consequently. The Craftsman stage should have caught these. Kill any that remain.

Sycophantic tone. Overly polite, eager-to-please phrasing. Excessive agreeableness. Rewrite with authority.

Sentence-length uniformity. All sentences roughly the same length and cadence. Human writing varies naturally. Short punchy sentences alongside longer ones. If the draft hums instead of pulses, vary.

False ranges. "From intimate gatherings to global movements." "From technical expertise to creative vision." Two loosely related things dressed as a spectrum. Name the specific things.

The "Challenges" formula. "Despite its [positive words], [subject] faces challenges..." followed by vague resolution. A rigid formula LLMs apply regardless of context. If challenges are relevant, describe them specifically.

Copula avoidance. AI replaces "is" and "are" with fancier constructions: "serves as," "stands as," "represents," "marks," "boasts," "features," "offers." A thing that IS something should be called what it IS. "Gallery 825 is LAAA's exhibition space" not "Gallery 825 serves as LAAA's exhibition space."

Dangling significance. Present participle phrases (-ing) stapled to the end of sentences to inject fake importance: "...highlighting its commitment to innovation," "...underscoring the significance of," "...reflecting broader trends in." These are superficial analysis disguised as observation. Delete or replace with a specific claim.

Legacy inflation. AI inserts statements about how something "marks a pivotal moment," "sets the stage for," "represents a shift," "symbolizes the ongoing," "reflects broader trends." It does this for even the most mundane topics. If the importance is real, prove it with evidence. If it's filler, cut it.

Vague attributions. "Experts argue," "Industry observers note," "Several publications have cited." AI invents consensus where none exists. Name the expert, or cut the attribution.

Elegant variation. AI avoids repeating a word by cycling through synonyms: the company, the firm, the organization, the enterprise. In business writing, repeat the word. Clarity over elegance.

"One of the most" formula. "One of the most influential," "one of the most important," "one of the most innovative." Threadbare and almost always unsubstantiated. State the specific claim instead.

## Final Check

After the pattern scan, read the entire draft once more as a skeptical reader.

Does this sound like one person wrote it? Would I believe a human wrote this? Is there a point of view, or does it read like a summary emptied of opinion? Does anything feel like it was written to sound smart rather than to say something? Is there a single sentence I could cut without losing meaning? If yes, cut it.
